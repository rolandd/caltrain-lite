## 2026-04-24 - Iterator and Array Allocation Overhead in Hot Loops
**Learning:** In the performance-critical GTFS-RT parser, implicit allocations from iterator functions (like `.find()`, `.reduce()`, and `for...of` iteration over Sets or Map iterators) present a notable performance penalty due to frequent GC churn when called hundreds of thousands of times per second.
**Action:** When working on parser hot paths (e.g. `gtfs-rt.ts`), favor classical `for` loops indexed by length and lazy array initializations (e.g., `s = s || []; s.push(val)`) over higher order functions to minimize implicit array/object allocations and closures. However, DO NOT manually construct iterators (like `const pIter = map.entries(); pIter.next()`) as it degrades code readability and violates constraint rules.
